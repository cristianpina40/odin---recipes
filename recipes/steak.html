Steak

Steak is a common dish that can be served with many different sides.

Steak is usually made with beef, but it can be made with other meat, such as deer meat.

Ingredients

  • 2 1 1/2 to 2-inch-thick ribeye, strip, porterhouse, or T-bone steaks (about 1 pound/450g each), or 4 tenderloin steaks (6 to 8 ounces/170 to 225g each)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 sprigs thyme or rosemary (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional)
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) butter (if pan-searing; optional)

Steps

  1. Add vegetable, canola, or rice bran oil to a heavy cast iron or stainless steel skillet, then place over the hottest burner you have and preheat skillet until it starts to smoke.
  2. Gently lay steak in skillet, using your fingers or a set of tongs. If desired, add a tablespoon of butter; for a cleaner-tasting sear, omit the butter at this stage.
  3. After 15 to 30 seconds, flip steak so that the second side comes into contact with the pan. Repeat, flipping steak every 15 to 30 seconds, until it has developed a nice brown sear
  4. Transfer cooked steak to a cutting board or serving platter and serve immediately.
